<p class="noindent"><h5>**Wednesday November 23rd: Read *Hillbilly Elegy* **</h5></p> 
If you haven't already, please start reading J.D. Vance's *Hillbily Elegy* so you can discuss it with us at our [Reading Through It book club](http://www.seattlereviewofbooks.com/notes/2016/11/15/reading-through-it-join-us-for-a-discussion-of-how-to-move-forward-in-donald-trumps-america/), which happens on December 7th at Third Place Books Seward Park.


<p class="noindent"><h5>**Thursday November 24th: Happy Thanksgiving!**</h5></p> 
Eat, drink, and try not to think about who's going to be president in less than two months.
 
<p class="noindent"><h5>**Friday November 25th: Read a Book You Already Own**</h5></p> 
Check out of all the Black Friday nonsense by staying home with a book you've bought but not read. Think of it as past you buying present you a gift. Don't go to a mall. Stay home and relax.

<p class="noindent"><h5>**Saturday November 26th: Indies First Party Bus**</h5></p> 
 
 See our Event of the Week column for more details.
*Various locations. Free. All ages. Noon – 5 p.m.*


<h5>**Sunday November 27th: Visit a Library**</h5></p> 
We still live in a country in which public libraries are open and available to the public. I mean, really: why *wouldn't* you visit a public library?



<h5>**Monday November 28th: *LOUD IDIOTS* Reading**</h5></p> 
Baltimore poet and short story writer Sarah Jean Alexander reads in Seattle for the very first time to celebrate her new book, *LOUD IDIOTS*. She’s joined by two of the guiding lights of the soon-to-be-nonexistent APRIL Festival, Frances Chiem and Willie Fitzgerald, along with Seattle-area writer Richard Chiem. 
*Vermillion,  1508 11th Ave, http://vermillionseattle.com/. Free. All ages. 7 p.m. *

<p class="noindent"><h5>**Tuesday November 29th: Two Sarahs Read Poetry**</h5></p>

Sarah Riggs has written five books of poetry and she’s also also directed films, including *Six Lives: A Cinepoem*. Riggs has come all the way from New York City to celebrate the latest book of poetry by Seattle author Sarah Mangold, the excellently titled *Giraffes of Devotion*.  *Open Books, 2414 N. 45th St., 633-0811, openpoetrybooks.com. Free. All ages. 7 p.m.*
