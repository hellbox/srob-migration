<h5>The Discovery of America by Christopher Columbus</h5>

You<br>
Salvador Dali<br>
see our history's golden man<br>
Christopher Columbus<br>
in your Andalusia way<br>
as robust young boy<br>
perhaps teenaged<br>
as one who pulls his big bellied ship<br>
toward us from ocean's edge<br>
masts&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;banners&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;swords&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;clouds<br>
crucifixes&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;shadows&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;sails<br>
monks&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;trapeze artists

Your<br>
huge canvas wall-sized<br>
brings home to Florida<br>
in this museum created<br>
in your name for you<br>
the wishes of those monarchs<br>
the youngish Ferdinand<br>
his lovely Isabella<br>
your own country<br>
the infant Spain

He<br>
Columbus not old<br>
enough to shave<br>
barefoot and shirtless<br>
not yet Great Admiral<br>
of the Ocean<br>
now drags ashore<br>
the Old World's wettest dreams<br>
of church<br>
of colony<br>
of empire<br>
that bid us marvel<br>
at our own demise

<h5>Blue Note Belly Blues — A Call and Response Poem</h5>

You came<br>
halfway<br>
round<br>
the world<br>
in the belly<br>
of a ship<br>
in the belly<br>
of a slave<br>
in the belly<br>
of the ocean

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

in the belly<br>
of a drum<br>
in the belly<br>
of a calabash<br>
in the belly<br>
of a dance<br>
in the belly<br>
of a shout<br>
bathed in<br>
palm oil

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

in the belly<br>
of a shuffle<br>
in the belly<br>
of a moan<br>
in the belly<br>
of a grin<br>
in the belly<br>
of a storm

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

in the belly<br>
of a savanna<br>
in the belly<br>
of the wind<br>
in the belly<br>
of the tides

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

Say Baby<br>
let's you &amp; me<br>
bust free<br>
ride<br>
this blue blue note<br>
into Freedom's belly<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

This blue note<br>
North Star<br>
gotta get outah here blue note<br>
this Black Bottom blue note<br>
this moaning low speaking low<br>
blue note<br>
this jazz back juke joint<br>
saxophone blue note

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

This high stepping 2nd line<br>
blue&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;blue&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;blue note<br>
this high flying Take me<br>
to the Moon<br>
blue note

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;Think About It

This Baby Baby Baby<br>
what have I done<br>
to be so black &amp; blue<br>
blue blue note<br>
let's kick back<br>
three ways to Sunday<br>
blue note<br>
blue note<br>
blue note

Baby<br>
let's turn ourselves inside out<br>
ride Freedom's blue<br> 
blue note<br>
and fly away<br>
fly away Baby

<h5>Ancestors</h5>

She drew her first basket<br>
on its side she made a mask<br>
w/ eyes and nose<br>
on its top she made a mouth<br>
slightly open<br>
she thought

She invited some friends<br>
to see the basket

Inside its curved belly<br>
they saw<br>
a flag w/ zebra marks<br>
the curved teeth of a lion<br>
gold&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;elegant&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;huge<br>
rows of elephant tusks<br>
a crocodile napping

They heard a spirit voice<br>
calling all their names

They struggled to breathe<br>
they tried to speak<br>
they wanted to dance

_(for Nisi)_
