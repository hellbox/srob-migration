The backwards map

You might find it hard to keep your footing if you charge at the poems in Ted Powers’s new collection Manners in the conventional way. Breaking into the first line and barreling straight down to the bottom doesn’t work. If you read these poems from beginning to end, it’s easy to get lost and turned around, until you start chasing after yourself in a recursive loop. I find it’s easier to flip through Manners, to cast your eyes about and wait for them to hook onto something muscular and twitchy. ... Read more
