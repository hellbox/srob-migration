The Sunday Post for August 9, 2015

[How to spot whodunnit: academics crack Agatha Christie's code](http://www.theguardian.com/books/2015/aug/02/academics-unlock-formula-agatha-christies-mysteries) At the bequest of television channel, a group of academics looked at Agatha Christie’s work, and developed formulas to reveal the patterns within. Many of the results concerned the gender of the killer. For example, they found that if the victim was strangled, the killer was more likely to be male (or male with a female accomplice), whereas if the setting was a country house – not uncommon for a Christie novel – there was a 75% chance the killer would be female. ...
