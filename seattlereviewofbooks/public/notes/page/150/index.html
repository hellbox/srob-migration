Fantagraphics author withdraws name from sexist award long list

This morning, the comics internet blew up when the long list for this year’s prestigious French comics award, the Grand Prix d’Angouleme, was announced. The list of 30 candidates did not include one single female cartoonist. And in case your first instinct was to mansplain this fact away — ”durr, but maybe no woman produced a comic worthy of awards this year,” I can hear the gamergate crowd grumbling, as though that made any sense at all — you should bear in mind that in the Grand Prix’s 43 years, only one woman has ever won the award. ... Congratulations to Ambassador Yang

Cartoonist Gene Luen Yang, who is best known for the excellent memoir American Born Chinese, has been named the first Ambassador for Young People’s Literature by the Library of Congress. He’ll be great at this gig, and it’s wonderful that the LoC is promoting literature to young people.
