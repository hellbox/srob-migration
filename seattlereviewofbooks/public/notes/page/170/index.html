At Dungeness Spit

Rushing toward the ocean tongue Along the lip of land I am what my friends feared Grit traded in for grains of sand My city soil scattered in Northwest winds Twice despised for behavior unbecoming— Unable to leave the cascade light Unwilling to claim the shadowed coo of pigeon’s songs as my only note.
