Seattle's literary magazines are kicking ass and taking names

Today, local literary magazine The James Franco Review, which treats every submission as though it was written by James Franco, started rolling out an “Emergency Issue” on art and engagment. What does that mean? Writers, editors, and artists around the country explored what it meant for them to be politically or consciously engaged in their work and to also examine literature’s relationship to safety.
