The Sunday Post for September 25, 2016

What We're Saying When We Talk About Hillary Clinton's "Likability" Sady Doyle unpacks the gendered responses to the first woman to be a major party candidate. No, Hillary Clinton is not a flawless person — or politician. You can complain about her secrecy, her hawkishness, or her husband all for good reason. But Clinton is, according to available biographical evidence, normal. Ordinary, even. She’s like countless women of her generation: Caught between the second wave of feminism and the marital norms of a pre-feminist age, driven to prove herself but cautious about seeming “pushy” or radical, aspiring to lead the nation into the 21st century yet baffled by her own fax machine. ...
