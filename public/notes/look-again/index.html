This week, the Seattle Review of Books is sponsored by Lois Farfel Stark — the award-winning filmmaker behind more than forty documentaries and, now, the author of The Telling Image. Stark’s new book is an exploration of how humans experience the world through shape: a web, a ladder, a helix. Her vast experience makes a tour de force out of what could easily have been a quiet coffee table book.

We look at the world in a sleepy way, especially through the lens of social media, where images slide past in a blur. Stark’s book is like caffeine for the visual center of your brain. Stop by our sponsorship page for a glimpse at the rich images she’s captured while traveling the world, and her take on them. Then go for a long walk. Keep your eyes open. See what you see.
